If you're building a WordPress store to sell digital products — ebooks, software, music, design assets, courses, or templates — you'll quickly encounter two dominant options: WooCommerce and Easy Digital Downloads (EDD). Both are mature, widely-used plugins with strong ecosystems. But they were built with different philosophies, and that matters for how well each fits your specific situation.

This is an honest comparison of both platforms to help you make the right choice.

## The Core Difference in Design Philosophy

**WooCommerce** was built as a general-purpose e-commerce platform. It handles physical products, digital products, subscriptions, and virtually anything you can sell online. Digital downloads are one of many product types it supports.

**Easy Digital Downloads** was built specifically and exclusively for selling digital products. Everything about it — the checkout flow, the license management, the reporting, the API — is designed around the particular needs of digital product sellers.

This philosophical difference shows up throughout every comparison below.

## Feature Comparison

### Digital File Delivery

**EDD:** File delivery is a first-class feature. You can sell multiple files per product, set download limits, configure link expiration, and control access for every product out of the box. The purchase receipt includes download links automatically, styled cleanly.

**WooCommerce:** Supports downloadable products natively, but it's clearly an add-on to a system built for physical goods. Basic file delivery works fine, but features like download limits, secure file hosting, and link expiration require more configuration.

**Winner:** EDD — purpose-built for this.

### License Management

If you're selling software with license keys, EDD's Software Licensing extension is the gold standard — it generates and validates licenses, handles renewals, and integrates with your application's activation system. This is one of the main reasons EDD dominates the WordPress plugin/theme marketplace space.

WooCommerce has third-party license management plugins, but none match EDD's software licensing integration in maturity.

**Winner:** EDD (especially for software).

### Subscription Products

**WooCommerce Subscriptions** (the premium WooCommerce extension) is extremely powerful — handling recurring payments, free trials, upgrade/downgrade flows, and most payment gateways. It's arguably the best subscription platform in the WordPress ecosystem.

**EDD Recurring Payments** handles subscriptions adequately for most use cases, but WooCommerce Subscriptions has more advanced features and better payment gateway support.

**Winner:** WooCommerce Subscriptions (for complex subscription needs).

### Physical Products (If You Ever Need Them)

If you sell primarily digital products but occasionally ship a physical item — a printed book alongside a PDF, merchandise, a USB drive — WooCommerce handles this natively. EDD does not support physical shipping at all.

**Winner:** WooCommerce (no contest).

### Checkout Experience

EDD's checkout is streamlined specifically for digital purchases — no shipping fields, no unnecessary steps. For a digital-only store, this lean checkout converts better because it removes friction irrelevant to the purchase.

WooCommerce's default checkout includes shipping fields that must be hidden or removed for digital-only stores. This is doable but adds configuration complexity.

**Winner:** EDD for digital-only stores; WooCommerce for mixed stores.

### Reporting and Analytics

Both plugins provide sales reporting, earnings by product, payment history, and customer records. EDD's built-in reporting is slightly more tailored to digital product metrics (downloads per product, license usage). WooCommerce's reporting is comprehensive and integrates with Google Analytics, WooCommerce Analytics, and numerous third-party tools.

**Winner:** Tie — both are solid; WooCommerce has a larger analytics ecosystem.

## Plugin Ecosystems

### WooCommerce

WooCommerce has an enormous ecosystem — thousands of plugins, payment gateways, and themes specifically built for it. Almost any functionality you can imagine has a WooCommerce plugin. Stripe, PayPal, Mollie, Klarna, Apple Pay — virtually every payment gateway supports WooCommerce.

### Easy Digital Downloads

EDD has a focused extension library (around 130 official extensions) targeting digital sellers specifically: software licensing, PDF stamping, content restriction, commissions for multi-vendor, course management integration. The ecosystem is smaller but more purposeful.

Both support Stripe and PayPal. WooCommerce supports more obscure gateways.

**Winner:** WooCommerce on total volume; EDD wins for digital-specific extensions.

## Pricing

Both plugins are free at their core. Costs come from extensions.

**WooCommerce:** Free core. Premium extensions from WooCommerce.com typically run €79–€199/year each. Third-party WooCommerce plugins range widely.

**Easy Digital Downloads:** Three tiers:
- Personal: ~€99/year (1 site, core extensions)
- Extended: ~€199/year (2 sites, more extensions)
- Professional: ~€299/year (unlimited sites, all extensions)

EDD's bundle pricing is often more economical for digital sellers who need multiple features. WooCommerce costs can escalate quickly if you need subscriptions, bookings, and advanced features each as separate purchases.

**Winner on total cost:** EDD for digital sellers (usually), WooCommerce for general stores.

## Ease of Setup

For a developer or technically comfortable user, both are manageable. For beginners:

WooCommerce has a polished setup wizard and extensive documentation. The sheer breadth of options can be overwhelming, but the guided setup covers the basics well.

EDD is lean and focused. There's less to configure because it has fewer use cases to support. Most digital sellers find EDD simpler to get operational quickly.

**Winner on simplicity:** EDD for digital-only sellers.

## My Recommendation

Choose **Easy Digital Downloads** if:
- You sell exclusively digital products (ebooks, software, plugins, themes, music, design assets)
- Software licensing is important to your business
- You want a streamlined, distraction-free checkout
- You want a platform designed specifically for your use case

Choose **WooCommerce** if:
- You sell a mix of physical and digital products
- You need advanced subscription management (WooCommerce Subscriptions is best-in-class)
- Your payment gateway isn't supported by EDD
- You're building a complex store that may expand in scope over time
- Your ecosystem of third-party integrations (CRM, shipping, fulfillment) is WooCommerce-specific

Both are excellent choices. The decision comes down to whether you're building a digital-only storefront (EDD's territory) or a versatile e-commerce platform that happens to include digital products (WooCommerce's territory).
