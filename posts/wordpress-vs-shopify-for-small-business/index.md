Every small business owner building an online store eventually faces this question: WordPress (with WooCommerce) or Shopify? Both are legitimate, widely-used platforms — but they make very different trade-offs, and the right choice depends heavily on your specific situation.

This is an honest comparison, not a sponsored opinion. Here's what actually matters.

## The Core Difference in Philosophy

Shopify is a hosted, all-in-one SaaS platform. You pay a monthly subscription, and Shopify handles hosting, security, updates, and payment processing infrastructure. You work within their system.

WordPress + WooCommerce is self-hosted open-source software. You own everything — the code, the data, the infrastructure — but you're also responsible for everything. It requires more technical knowledge (or more developer budget) to set up and maintain.

This fundamental difference drives most of the trade-offs below.

## Cost Comparison

### Shopify
- Basic plan: ~€32/month
- Shopify plan: ~€92/month
- Shopify Payments transaction fee: 0% (using their processor); 2% if using external payment gateway
- Apps: Many essential functions (subscriptions, advanced filtering, loyalty programs) require paid apps, often €10–€50/month each

A realistic Shopify setup for a growing small business typically costs €80–€200/month in platform and app fees alone.

### WordPress + WooCommerce
- WooCommerce core: free
- WordPress hosting: €15–€50/month for quality managed hosting
- Domain: ~€15/year
- Premium theme or custom development: one-time cost
- Plugins: Many premium plugins are €50–€200/year, not monthly

WordPress costs more upfront but often less over time. A site that costs €3,000 to build professionally might have ongoing costs of €200–€400/year. Run the numbers over three to five years and WooCommerce often comes out significantly cheaper.

**Winner on long-term cost:** WordPress/WooCommerce (usually)

## Ease of Use

Shopify's dashboard is polished and designed for non-technical users. Adding products, processing orders, and managing basic settings is genuinely easier in Shopify — especially for complete beginners.

WooCommerce is not difficult once set up properly, but the setup itself requires either technical knowledge or professional help. Managing updates, troubleshooting plugin conflicts, and handling server-related issues are things Shopify completely removes from your plate.

If you want to do everything yourself with zero technical background, Shopify wins here. If you're working with a developer or have some technical comfort, WordPress catches up quickly.

**Winner on ease of use:** Shopify (for non-technical users)

## Flexibility and Customization

This is where WordPress/WooCommerce has a decisive advantage. WordPress is open source — you can modify anything, build any custom functionality, and integrate with virtually any third-party system. There are no artificial limitations on what you can do.

Shopify is flexible within its ecosystem, but you are fundamentally working within their constraints. Custom checkout modifications, for example, require Shopify Plus (€2,300+/month). Certain features simply cannot be built on Shopify without their highest tier.

If your business has non-standard requirements — custom pricing logic, complex B2B features, specific integration with your internal systems, or unusual product configurations — WooCommerce handles them; Shopify may not.

**Winner on flexibility:** WordPress/WooCommerce (no contest)

## SEO Capabilities

Both platforms can achieve excellent search rankings. The idea that one is inherently better for SEO than the other is largely a myth. What matters is how well the platform is configured and how good the content is.

That said, WordPress with the right setup has some practical SEO advantages:
- More granular control over URLs, breadcrumbs, schema markup
- Better plugin ecosystem (Yoast, RankMath) with deeper configuration
- No forced `/collections/` or `/products/` URL structure like Shopify imposes
- Easier to implement technical SEO changes without hitting platform limits

**Winner on SEO:** WordPress (marginal advantage with proper setup)

## Security and Maintenance

Shopify handles all security patches, hosting infrastructure, and PCI compliance for payment processing. You never need to worry about updating software, patching vulnerabilities, or managing server configuration.

WordPress requires active maintenance. If you don't keep WordPress core, themes, and plugins updated, security vulnerabilities accumulate. A neglected WordPress site is a target. However, a properly maintained and hardened WordPress installation is perfectly secure — it's the neglect that creates problems.

If you don't want to think about maintenance, Shopify is genuinely the better choice. If you're willing to pay for a maintenance plan or handle it yourself, WordPress is fine.

**Winner on maintenance burden:** Shopify

## Data Ownership and Portability

This is perhaps the most underappreciated factor in the long term. With WordPress, your site lives on your hosting account. All your data, code, and content belongs to you completely. Moving to a different host or developer is straightforward.

With Shopify, your store lives on their infrastructure. If Shopify changes their pricing, discontinues your plan, or you decide to leave, exporting your data is possible but moving the actual store experience elsewhere is significant work. You are a tenant on their platform.

For businesses building something they expect to operate for many years, data ownership matters.

**Winner on ownership:** WordPress (definitively)

## My Recommendation

Choose **Shopify** if:
- You're a complete beginner who needs to launch quickly with no technical help
- Your store has standard product types and a straightforward checkout
- You want zero infrastructure responsibility
- You're selling less than €50,000/year and want simplicity

Choose **WordPress + WooCommerce** if:
- You have custom functionality requirements
- Long-term cost matters to you
- You want full ownership of your platform and data
- You're working with a developer who can handle setup and maintenance
- Your business has complex product configurations, wholesale pricing, or specific integration needs

Neither is wrong. But for a growing business with ambitions beyond a basic catalog, WooCommerce's flexibility and lower long-term cost make it the more powerful choice — as long as you're willing to invest in proper setup and maintenance.
