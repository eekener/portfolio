A restaurant's website has one primary job: turn a visitor who found you online into someone who walks through your door (or places an order). Every design decision and feature should serve that goal. Yet most restaurant websites are cluttered with things visitors don't need and missing the things that would actually convert them.

This guide covers what a modern, effective WordPress restaurant website should include — and what it can skip.

## The Non-Negotiables

### 1. Your Menu — Easy to Read, Easy to Find

Your menu is the most important page on your restaurant's website. Visitors come specifically to see what you serve. Yet many restaurant menus are:
- Buried three clicks deep in the navigation
- Presented as PDFs that don't load well on mobile
- Never updated (still showing last season's specials from two years ago)

Your WordPress menu page should be built in HTML — not uploaded as a PDF. HTML menus are:
- Readable on all devices without downloading
- Searchable by Google (a "gluten-free pasta Braunschweig" search can lead to your menu page)
- Fast to update when dishes or prices change

Organize the menu logically: starters, mains, desserts, drinks. Show prices. If you have dietary options (vegan, gluten-free, etc.), mark them clearly with icons. Hungry people scan menus; make scanning easy.

### 2. An Obvious Phone Number and Address

Put your phone number and address in the header of every page — not just the contact page. Visitors often grab a phone number and leave; make it effortless to find. The phone number should be a clickable `tel:` link on mobile so visitors can call with one tap.

Include a Google Maps embed on the contact page, but also write out the full street address as text — screen readers, GPS apps, and copy-paste all work better with a real address than with an embedded map.

### 3. Opening Hours — Current and Correct

Outdated opening hours might be the single most damaging thing a restaurant website can display. If your site says you're open on Mondays and you're not, that's a customer who drove to a closed restaurant and will not return.

Show your hours prominently, update them for holidays, and if your hours change seasonally, build a system for keeping them current. A WordPress custom field with hours in an easily-editable format is better than hardcoded text in a template.

### 4. Online Reservation System

If you take reservations, an online booking system dramatically reduces phone workload and makes it easy for customers to reserve at any hour. Integration options for WordPress include:

- **OpenTable** — high traffic from the OpenTable app, but takes a per-cover fee
- **Resy** — increasingly popular, restaurant-friendly pricing
- **Formitable** — popular in Europe, integrates well
- **Simple plugins** like Restaurant Reservations (WP Restaurant Manager) — lower cost, self-managed
- **A simple contact form** — works fine for small restaurants with limited reservation volume

The best option depends on your volume and budget. For most independent restaurants, a direct integration with a booking platform (so reservations sync with one system) beats managing a custom form.

### 5. Food Photography That Makes People Hungry

In an era where people discover restaurants through Instagram and Google Images, your food photography determines whether someone books or scrolls past. Professional food photography is expensive but worth it for most restaurants.

If you can't invest in professional photography yet:
- Shoot in natural daylight, never with flash
- Clean, simple backgrounds (white surface, natural wood)
- Shoot from above or at a 45-degree angle
- Use the portrait mode on a modern smartphone — it's genuinely good
- Edit lightly: brightness, contrast, and color temperature only

Never use stock photos of food. Visitors can tell, and it destroys trust.

## Features That Add Real Value

### Online Ordering (For Delivery/Takeaway)

If you offer delivery or takeaway, your WordPress site can handle online ordering in several ways:

- **Third-party integration** (Uber Eats, Lieferando, Deliveroo): Easy to set up, but high commission fees (15–30%)
- **WooCommerce with a food ordering plugin**: You keep the margins, but requires setup and payment processing
- **Direct integration with your POS system**: Ideal for volume, but more complex

For independent restaurants serious about margins, having direct ordering on your own site — even if you keep the big platforms too for discovery — saves significant money per order.

### Blog or News Section

A simple news section for sharing seasonal menus, events, new dishes, or staff stories serves two purposes: it gives existing customers a reason to return to your site, and it produces the fresh, local-interest content that helps with Google rankings.

Keep it simple. A post once a month is enough. Photos from your kitchen, a seasonal menu announcement, or an upcoming event promotion all work well.

### Social Media Integration

Your Instagram feed, if it's active and full of good food photos, is worth embedding on your homepage. It provides fresh visual content automatically and signals to visitors that the restaurant is active. Keep the implementation lightweight — many social feed plugins load too much JavaScript.

## What Restaurant Websites Usually Don't Need

**Flash animations, fancy intro pages, or background music** — they slow your site down and annoy users. Visitors came for your menu, not an experience.

**10+ pages of content** — most restaurants need: Home, Menu, About, Reservations/Contact, and maybe a Gallery. That's it.

**Heavy slider carousels with 8 images** — a single beautiful hero image loads faster and converts better than an autoplay carousel. Use a carousel only if you have genuinely distinct, high-quality content to cycle through.

**A complex blog with weekly posts** — only if you'll actually maintain it. An abandoned blog with posts from 2022 looks worse than no blog at all.

## Mobile Experience Is Everything

Over 70% of restaurant website traffic comes from mobile — typically someone out in the city, trying to decide where to eat right now, or checking if you're open. Your site must work flawlessly on a phone.

Must-haves for mobile:
- Large, tappable phone number in the header
- Menu that's readable without zooming
- Reservation booking that works on a small screen
- Maps integration that opens the native maps app on tap
- Page load time under 3 seconds on a mobile connection

Test your site yourself on your own phone regularly. Eat at the experience your customers are having.

## Local SEO for Restaurants

Your website also needs to help people find you on Google when they search "Italian restaurant Braunschweig" or "lunch near me."

Key local SEO steps:
- **Claim and complete your Google Business Profile** — this is more important than your website for local search
- **Consistent NAP** — Name, Address, Phone number must be identical across your website, Google, Yelp, and all directories
- **Local keywords in page content** — naturally include your city name in your homepage, about page, and menu descriptions
- **Schema markup** — structured data for restaurants tells Google your hours, menu URL, price range, and cuisine type

A WordPress site with proper local SEO setup, combined with an active and complete Google Business Profile, can put an independent restaurant on the first page of local search results.

A great restaurant WordPress site isn't about having the most features — it's about removing every barrier between a hungry visitor and a table at your restaurant.
